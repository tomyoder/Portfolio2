About me

I started playing the trombone when I was nine and it has really shaped most of my life. I have a band How Things Work, which is an indie rock band with:

  • A Singer
  • Drums
  • Bass
  • andtwo horns
instead of the usual guitar.

Trying to make a living as a trombonist meant I had to pick up a lot of other skills in order to pay the rent. I've spent the last twenty years working as a

  1. Welder
  2. Machinist
  3. Mechanic
as well as recording location sound for film.

Playing the trombone in rock bands meant I had to build my own gear. If you play a guitar, you go to Guitar Center. There is no Trombone Center. I built my own amps and equipment so I could go onstage and plug in and play through a stack like any self-respecting guitarist. The photo above is of a microphone I made that goes to a preamp system that gives a proper signal to the club sound board from both horns. I love building this stuff, almost as much as playing the horn.

This emphasais on function, design, and construction is what led me to pursue programming. I am very interested in the underlying structure of software and how the machine actually works.
